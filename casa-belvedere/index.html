The Story

Under the gaze of the Madonna di Belvedere

Casa Belvedere sits in the Belvedere countryside outside Carovigno, on a gentle hill that slopes toward the Adriatic. The sea is close enough to walk to in the morning, but the house is set back among the olive groves, in the quiet.

On the corner of the property, where the road bends, there is a small weathered cappelletta — a roadside shrine, whitewashed and topped with twin crosses. Inside is the Madonna di Belvedere, the patron saint of Carovigno, whose sanctuary on the hill gives this whole stretch of countryside its name. She has been watching over this road for longer than anyone here can remember.

The view from the rooftop terrace is the bel vedere she has been watching over all along.

The House

Built to live in, not just to photograph

We have stayed in enough beautiful Pugliese houses to know what they get wrong: rooms that look perfect in pictures and feel uncomfortable in person. Casa Belvedere is the opposite. The scale is right. The furniture is sat on. The kitchen is used.

La Pianta · The Plan

Tutto su un solo livello

Casa Belvedere si sviluppa interamente su un piano: la zona giorno e la zona notte si affacciano su una corte interna con un ulivo secolare, e tutto si apre verso la piscina e la veranda. Sopra, la terrazza panoramica copre quasi l’intera superficie della casa.

Everything on one level: the living and sleeping wings face an internal courtyard with a centuries-old olive tree, and open out to the pool and covered veranda. Above, the panoramic rooftop terrace covers nearly the entire footprint of the house.

The Rooftop

The bel vedere

A staircase from the courtyard climbs to the roof. The terrace covers nearly the entire footprint of the house — a second floor entirely in the open air, with uninterrupted views east to the Adriatic. A thin blue line in the morning, deeper through the afternoon, gold at sunset.

It is the best place on the property for a long breakfast, a quiet aperitivo, or an early swim before the light gets sharp.

The Land

Olive groves, the shrine, the road to the sea

Photo — The cappelletta on the property corner

The property is surrounded by olive trees, some of them several centuries old. The land is bordered by a low stone wall built in the old way, with the cappelletta set into the corner where the road bends toward the sea.

The beach is a short drive — or, for the unhurried, a long walk through the macchia. The Riserva di Torre Guaceto is a few minutes north: protected coastline, wild dunes, and some of the cleanest water on the Adriatic.
